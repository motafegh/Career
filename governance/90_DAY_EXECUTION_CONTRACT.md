# 90-Day Execution Contract

**Status:** Controlling  
**Preparation:** 2026-07-18 to 2026-07-19  
**Execution:** 2026-07-20 to 2026-10-17  
**Duration:** 90 calendar days  
**Responsibility:** Stable workload, capacity, review, route-change, and anti-diversion commitments  
**Live state:** The canonical tracker controls current milestone, gate, blocker, and next responsibility. This contract does not record session-level state.

## 1. Commitment

For the full execution period, Ali follows the approved route, priorities, deliverables, gates, and work rules recorded in the repository.

The program is intentionally directive because open choices, repeated redesign, padded schedules, and prolonged planning have previously displaced execution.

This contract does not transfer personal responsibility to an AI. Ali retains authority over health, safety, legality, finances, privacy, credentials, and whether an instruction is safe to execute.

Operational meaning:

- Python/data engineering is the primary implementation capability.
- Machine learning is the analytical specialization.

- Systems/Linux/networking are learned when required by the product.
- Distributed, cloud, agentic, orchestration, and MLOps systems follow the existing approved strategy and exposure policy.

UpgradePilot is the single selected primary flagship. Broad project comparison is closed.

AegisLab, Sentinel, and other repositories remain historical evidence unless a formal approved decision assigns a bounded role. Their architecture, sequencing, modules, and tasks do not transfer automatically.

## 3. Exact workload requirement

### Standard production week

- Monday: **4 focused hours**
- Tuesday: **4 focused hours**
- Wednesday: **4 focused hours**
- Thursday: **4 focused hours**
- Friday: **4 focused hours**
- Saturday: **4 focused hours**
- Standard weekly minimum: **24 focused hours**
- Sunday: **30-minute closure and activation review**, not counted toward technical hours

Dog walking, general AI conversation, passive reading, videos, and planning count only when the active plan explicitly classifies them as focused work and they produce required evidence.

At least **3 of each Green day's 4 hours** must normally involve laptop/terminal implementation, evidence investigation, experiment, testing, debugging, data work, or technically necessary artifact production.

### Timebox interpretation

A time estimate is a ceiling, planning container, or maximum investigation budget. It is not an instruction to spend that amount on a trivial task.

When an item completes early:

1. verify the deliverable;
2. preserve required evidence;
3. continue to the next authorized item only when one is clearly available and cognitive quality remains adequate;
4. otherwise use safe remaining capacity under the cognitive-stop rule.

No task becomes substantial merely because it was assigned a large timebox.

## 4. Capacity modes

The mode is selected honestly at session start.

### Green mode

Condition: normal usable functioning.

Requirement: **4 focused hours**, normally two 120-minute blocks, unless an explicit earlier stop line is reached and no suitable authorized continuation is available.

### Yellow mode

Condition: poor sleep, marked grogginess, unstable concentration, or reduced but usable functioning.

Requirement: **2.5 focused hours** of low-risk execution. The missing **1.5 hours** becomes weekly recovery work.

Allowed work includes:

- evidence inspection and annotation;
- tests;
- fixture preparation;
- SQL practice tied to the active slice;
- documentation derived from completed behavior;
- small bounded repairs;
- parser/validation work that does not require consequential architecture changes.

No consequential architecture decision.

### Red mode

Condition: functioning is too impaired for safe, reliable technical work or there is a significant health/personal disruption.

Requirement: **0 technical hours**. Preserve state and resume the same controlled responsibility later. No unsafe compensatory overwork.

Repeated Red days trigger evaluator review, not punishment or an automatic plan replacement.

### Recovery rule

- Yellow-hour deficit is recovered within the same week when safe, normally on Saturday or by adding up to 60 minutes to later Green days.
- A Red day reduces the adjusted weekly minimum by 4 hours.
- Recovery work never exceeds **5 focused hours in one day**.
- Hours remain secondary to deliverables and evidence.

## 5. Cognitive stop rule

Do not begin a new consequential responsibility merely to fill remaining hours when concentration, comprehension, diagnostic quality, or safe judgment has materially declined.

Safe remaining capacity may be used for:

- reviewing already-written code;
- replaying established behavior;
- organizing evidence;
- delayed recall;
- bounded cleanup;
- a previously authorized low-risk continuation;
- closing the session with accurate state preservation.

This rule prevents poor-quality advancement; it does not authorize idle padding or routine early stopping.

## 6. Daily delivery rule

Every production day must end with one or more exact outputs named by the active plan or work package.

Acceptable outputs include:

- traceable evidence report;
- working behavior;
- passing or intentionally failing test with interpreted result;
- query and verified result;
- reproducible fixture;
- scenario contract;
- evidence map;
- parser/model/CLI behavior;
- diagnosed and repaired failure;
- committed technical artifact;
- reduced-prompt ownership demonstration;
- an authorized advanced-systems exposure artifact under the existing policy.

Time spent without a concrete result may still be necessary, but it must be labeled investigation and cannot silently satisfy a delivery gate.

## 7. Weekly and milestone gate rule

A milestone or week passes only when:

1. every required Must Deliver item exists;
2. named commands, tests, changed cases, or evidence gates pass;
3. evidence is committed or linked;
4. limitations and assistance are explicit;
5. Ali completes the required explanation, modification, query, test, or diagnosis check;
6. the next stage does not depend on an unacknowledged failure.

A passed gate ends optional polishing. Advancement occurs only to a named authorized deliverable and only when capacity and cognitive quality support it.

## 8. Anti-rabbit-hole rules

- One active primary project: UpgradePilot.
- One active milestone/work package at a time.
- During execution mode, one selected next action.
- Consequential unresolved choices may use decision mode.
- Material technical questions may use bounded exploration mode.
- Ordinary unrelated ideas receive a brief deferred note and reconsideration trigger.
- Existing approved advanced-systems strategy, capacity, and package requirements remain unchanged.
- Only one major advanced-systems exposure package is normally active at a time.
- Missing prerequisites receive bounded repair and review under the Session and Blocker Protocol.
- A technical blocker is investigated before several layers or evidence assumptions are changed.
- A missed day resumes at the same controlled responsibility.
- A failed week causes blocker repair or bounded scope adjustment, not an automatic new roadmap.
- A technology pilot does not silently become permanent architecture.
- Existing AegisLab or Sentinel work does not become active because it already exists.

## 9. Planning categories

Distinguish:

- **governance planning** — route, milestone, project allocation, or program redesign;
- **technical decision records** — ADRs, specification amendments, experiment protocols, or threat models required by consequential decisions;
- **execution sketches** — short current-step sequences, tests, evidence, and stop lines.

Anti-planning controls prohibit unnecessary governance planning. They do not prohibit normal engineering decomposition or warranted technical decision records.

## 10. Prohibited activities

Until Day 90, do not:

- reopen broad candidate comparison without formal change evidence;
- follow deferred AegisLab execution plans;
- create or restore implementation outside an authorized boundary;
- use removed scaffold or historical architecture proposals as accepted starting material;
- adopt a new career identity;
- start a general course unrelated to the active product, blocker, or authorized exposure package;
- add Kubernetes, microservices, distributed queues, multi-cloud deployment, autonomous multi-agent systems, or advanced MLOps ad hoc;
- adopt those technologies permanently without the existing approved baseline, evidence, cost, and decision requirements;
- perform advanced ML before a real question, defensible data/truth, and measured baseline exist;
- return to broad Sentinel participation;
- spend a full week only on plans, profiles, or presentation documentation;
- claim production readiness or professional mastery without evidence;
- inflate a small checkpoint into a full-day task;
- execute untrusted repository code merely to inspect a public case.

## 12. Change criteria

A master-route or primary-project change requires one of:

- demonstrated technical infeasibility;
- material external constraint;
- safety or legal issue;
- a prerequisite exceeding allocated scope materially;
- strong evidence at a formal review point;
- repeated proof that delivery density is materially too low or unrealistically high;
- repeated evidence that project design conflicts with Ali's documented learning mode;
- evidence that approved advanced-systems requirements cannot be satisfied credibly within the mission.

Any change must state evidence, affected gate, smallest necessary modification, what remains fixed, deadline/scope consequence, how existing work is preserved, how uncontrolled expansion is prevented, and how the revision will be tested.

## 13. Breach response

If Ali starts an unapproved competing project, returns to open-ended comparison, creates extra governance planning instead of executing, follows a deferred plan, pads trivial work, or repeatedly ignores the active order:

1. stop the competing, premature, or unbounded activity;
2. record what happened without moral judgment;
3. reopen the governing charter and current controlled artifact;
4. resume the first incomplete authorized deliverable;
5. diagnose recurrence after execution resumes.

No restart from Day 1 unless the program was materially interrupted or a formal review establishes a revised execution date.

## 14. Maintenance

Change this contract only when stable workload, capacity, review, route-change, or anti-diversion commitments change.

Do not add current milestone, method, session, exact-next-action, or implementation details.
