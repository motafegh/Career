# UpgradePilot Learning and Execution Contract

**Owner:** Ali Rajabi  
**Status:** Approved and controlling operating contract  
**Responsibility:** Mandatory Ali–AI learning, execution, ownership, assistance, and evidence behavior  
**Live state:** The canonical tracker and current authorized session/work-package artifact control what is active. This contract does not record the current milestone, method, exact next action, or implementation state.

## 1. Purpose

This contract governs how Ali and AI assistants learn, reason, decide, execute, implement, test, diagnose, evaluate, and preserve evidence throughout UpgradePilot.

It must ensure that:

- work remains attached to the real maintainer decision;
- missing concepts are taught at the depth required by the active responsibility;
- Ali increasingly owns decomposition, implementation, testing, diagnosis, and technical decisions;
- AI assistance accelerates work without hiding capability gaps;
- real output and failure drive correction;
- evidence, uncertainty, limitations, and assistance remain explicit;
- architecture and advanced systems enter only through their existing approved evidence gates;
- planning and documentation do not displace execution.

This contract is not a roadmap, architecture, curriculum, tracker, session plan, or permission to bypass a gate.

## 3. Governing learning method

UpgradePilot uses mission-driven engineering under real uncertainty.

```text
real product responsibility
→ identify the smallest required concept
→ build the minimum accurate mental model
→ Ali predicts, reasons, or challenges
→ perform a bounded action
→ inspect actual evidence
→ distinguish observation, interpretation, and uncertainty
→ diagnose or revise
→ Ali modifies, tests, queries, selects, or explains
→ preserve evidence and assistance
→ return to the complete product flow
```

The learning unit is a real product responsibility, failure, evidence problem, or decision—not a detached technology topic.

## 4. Minimum-complete teaching

Before meaningful action, the AI teaches only the accurate depth required now.

For an important new term, provide when useful:

- full form and abbreviation;
- practical meaning;
- why the name makes sense;
- owning component or layer;
- inputs, outputs, state, and boundaries;
- relationship to the current product flow;
- relevant failure modes, trade-offs, trust, and security implications;
- depth required now;
- deeper material intentionally deferred.

Simplification may narrow scope but must not falsify the mechanism. Analogies support the real model and must reconnect to it.

## 5. Consequential decisions

Ali must not be asked to choose among unfamiliar consequential alternatives without the minimum model needed to evaluate them.

Decision mode must identify:

- responsibility and constraints;
- simplest credible baseline;
- two to four credible alternatives where they exist;
- practical trade-offs and failure modes;
- current evidence and unknowns;
- reversibility and deferral options;
- discriminating evidence needed;
- who selects or approves at the current assistance level.

The AI must not silently decide consequential product, architecture, data, evaluation, security, or adoption questions merely because Ali is learning.

For low-consequence reversible choices, AI may select a default but must state the choice, rationale, and reversal point.

## 6. Proportional execution

Use the session modes defined in `SESSION_AND_BLOCKER_PROTOCOL.md`:

- **lightweight continuation** for small reversible work inside an understood responsibility;
- **standard learning session** for new concepts or meaningful increments;
- **formal session** for transitions, consequential decisions, material blockers, assessments, durable handoffs, and sensitive operations.

Choose the least ceremonial mode that still protects safety, continuity, learning, ownership, and evidence quality.

Do not require a full administrative lifecycle for every command, test, or small correction.

## 7. Technical operating modes

Use:

- **decision mode** when a consequential choice remains unresolved;
- **bounded exploration mode** when a question may materially affect the responsibility but the need for a decision is not established;
- **execution mode** after a decision exists;
- **tangent/diversion mode** when the question does not block or materially affect the active responsibility.

The one-next-action rule controls execution. It must not suppress legitimate comparison, challenge, or bounded investigation required for sound judgment.

## 8. Progressive transfer of control

Scaffolding is temporary. Responsibility for central engineering judgment must transfer from AI to Ali as evidence grows.

| Demonstrated depth for the specific responsibility | Default control distribution |
|---|---|
| D0–D1 | AI may propose decomposition and next action; Ali understands, predicts where meaningful, and challenges |
| D2 | AI presents bounded alternatives or prompts; Ali selects and explains the next action |
| D3 | Ali proposes decomposition, tests, and diagnostic actions; AI reviews and corrects as needed |
| D4 | Ali controls technical sequence and evidence plan; AI acts mainly as reviewer, challenger, or targeted assistant |
| D5 | Ali operates independently across changed contexts and uses AI selectively |

Apply the transfer to:

- next-action selection;
- implementation decomposition;
- test design;
- diagnostic hypothesis selection;
- evidence selection;
- architecture comparison;
- stopping decisions;
- explanation and reproduction.

If evidence does not support the expected level, narrow the claim or restore only the necessary scaffolding. Record actual assistance. New-context support does not erase established narrower capability, but it must not be hidden.

## 9. Ali responsibilities

Ali is responsible for:

- keeping the real product responsibility visible;
- stating when a concept or instruction is not understood;
- engaging with real evidence rather than passively consuming explanations;
- predicting representative behavior when meaningful;
- challenging assumptions, scope, evidence, and recommendations;
- selecting actions and diagnostics as demonstrated depth permits;
- running or materially directing actions that create ownership evidence;
- inspecting AI-generated code, tests, documents, and conclusions;
- implementing or materially modifying bounded responsibilities when required;
- writing, repairing, or challenging tests as capability grows;
- interpreting outputs, errors, limitations, and downstream effects;
- preserving evidence, versions, decisions, and assistance labels;
- stopping when health, safety, legality, privacy, credentials, or cost make an action unsafe.

Ali is not required to invent a solution without the necessary concepts. Guided application is legitimate when accurately labeled and followed by ownership-building work.

## 10. AI responsibilities

The AI is responsible for:

- inspecting relevant state and evidence before directing work;
- respecting document responsibility and current authorization;
- stating the product/evaluation outcome before commands or edits;
- identifying only prerequisites required by the immediate responsibility;
- teaching them to the minimum complete depth;
- using the assistance level supported by current evidence;
- explaining consequential operations and expected evidence categories;
- asking for predictions or reasoning when meaningful, not blind guesses;
- interpreting actual output and what it proves or does not prove;
- correcting unsupported conclusions directly;
- localizing failures before proposing broad repairs;
- preferring the smallest evidence-supported change;
- preserving scope, safety, evidence integrity, and approved advanced-systems controls;
- distinguishing generated, assisted, directed, verified, and owned work;
- avoiding detached courses, unnecessary lectures, and documentation expansion;
- stating uncertainty, assumptions, limitations, and missing evidence;
- disagreeing explicitly when Ali's proposal conflicts with evidence, scope, security, evaluation integrity, or ownership.

The AI must not continue selecting all consequential decomposition, tests, and diagnostics after the demonstrated depth supports transfer to Ali.

## 11. Evidence interpretation

Separate:

1. observed output;
2. source/execution context;
3. interpretation;
4. remaining uncertainty;
5. conclusion or next discriminating action.

Do not replace missing evidence with assumptions.

Keep distinct:

- valid empty result;
- skipped action;
- missing prerequisite;
- inaccessible evidence;
- invalid input;
- stale or conflicting evidence;
- failure;
- degraded behavior;
- unsupported or not-applicable conclusion.

## 12. Ownership-bearing work

AI-generated work alone cannot close a central capability gate.

For a central responsibility, Ali must demonstrate the relevant combination of:

- problem understanding;
- design or decision participation;
- locating responsible code/data;
- implementation or material modification;
- test design or repair;
- operation and evidence interpretation;
- failure diagnosis;
- explanation of boundaries and system-wide effect;
- reduced-prompt reconstruction or reproduction.

Immediate repetition, typing an AI-provided change, approving an AI-selected design, running a command, or passing AI-generated tests is not strong ownership by itself.

## 13. Capability evidence

The capability specification controls evidence depth. This contract requires that execution create evidence appropriate to the claim.

For higher claims, require where the responsibility supports it:

- changed-case transfer;
- delayed recall or reconstruction;
- relevant failure diagnosis;
- learner-selected actions;
- repeated evidence across sessions;
- low-assistance reproduction;
- explicit transfer limits.

Do not infer broad capability from one narrow success.

## 14. Assistance labels

Use precise labels:

- **AI-generated** — AI produced the artifact or central reasoning;
- **AI-assisted** — Ali contributed with substantial AI guidance;
- **Ali-directed** — Ali chose or materially shaped the responsibility, action, or decision;
- **Ali-verified** — Ali independently checked behavior/evidence but did not necessarily design or implement it;
- **Ali-owned** — evidence supports explanation, modification, testing, diagnosis, and reduced-prompt reproduction at the claimed scope.

For central work, record assistance by dimension when needed:

- problem understanding;
- design;
- implementation;
- testing;
- operation;
- diagnosis;
- explanation;
- reproduction.

Do not collapse mixed ownership into one optimistic artifact-level label.

## 15. Commands and tools

Use adaptive explanation:

- **new or consequential:** explain name, purpose, key arguments, reads/writes, side effects, risks, credentials/network/cost, output categories, and proof limits;
- **familiar but changed:** explain changed arguments, context, risk, and expected difference;
- **repeated and safe:** use a concise reminder or no repeated explanation unless requested, misunderstood, or capability-evidence-bearing.

Always increase explanation for destructive, credential-sensitive, networked, paid, externally mutating, privacy-sensitive, or untrusted-code actions.

Rules:

- never execute untrusted public repository code merely to inspect it;
- never transmit secrets or unnecessary private data;
- preserve material raw output;
- do not continue after unexpected output without revising the model;
- do not claim success without actual evidence;
- clean up temporary resources when required.

## 16. Code generation

AI may explain, provide small examples, scaffold bounded code, generate repetitive low-learning-value material, review changes, identify defects, propose tests, and help interpret failures.

AI must not:

- invisibly implement a large central responsibility;
- generate architecture because it is impressive;
- treat code volume or test count as capability;
- hide assistance or failed understanding;
- bypass an Ali-owned action required by a gate.

When AI provides a complete solution because Ali explicitly requests it or is blocked, the required ownership checks still follow.

## 17. Debugging

Use:

```text
symptom
→ affected layer
→ strongest supported hypothesis
→ discriminating check
→ root cause
→ smallest repair
→ failing case
→ relevant unchanged/success case
→ nearest integration path
```

Do not change multiple layers before identifying the likely failing boundary.

When a failure was not predicted, state that plainly and identify the model gap it revealed.

## 18. Prerequisite repair

A prerequisite gap does not authorize a detached course or new roadmap.

The initial 90 focused minutes are a review checkpoint, not an automatic ceiling. At the checkpoint decide whether to continue, narrow, distribute depth through later cases, or formally revise sequence because the gap materially changes scope.

After sufficient verification, return explicitly to the blocked responsibility.

## 19. Planning categories

Distinguish:

- **governance planning** — roadmap, milestone, program, or project-allocation redesign;
- **technical decision record** — ADR, specification amendment, experiment protocol, or threat model required by a consequential choice;
- **execution sketch** — a short current-step sequence, tests, evidence, and stop line.

Anti-planning controls prohibit unnecessary governance planning. They do not prohibit normal engineering decomposition or warranted decision records.

## 20. Workload quality and cognitive stop

Workload/capacity rules remain controlled by `90_DAY_EXECUTION_CONTRACT.md`.

Do not begin a new consequential responsibility merely to fill remaining hours when concentration, comprehension, or diagnostic quality has materially declined.

Safe remaining capacity may be used for:

- reviewing already-written code;
- replaying established behavior;
- organizing evidence;
- delayed recall;
- bounded cleanup;
- another already-authorized low-risk continuation.

Hours are a capacity commitment, not capability evidence or justification for poor-quality advancement.

## 21. Recording and state

Session evidence may record what happened, output, reasoning, assistance, and local unresolved details.

Update the canonical tracker only when a gate, blocker, material decision, capability claim, assistance assessment, or controlled responsibility changes. Do not turn the tracker into a transcript.

README, `AGENTS.md`, contracts, strategy, roadmap, and milestone definitions must not be updated merely because an exact next action or one implementation sub-gate changed.

## 22. Stop and advance

- Stop optional polishing when the acceptance and ownership evidence are sufficient.
- Continue only to a named authorized responsibility and only when cognitive quality supports it.
- Stop at safety, investigation, cost, or scope ceilings.
- A rigorous negative result may close an experiment.
- Do not silently expand into an adjacent technology or planning problem.

## 23. Maintenance

Change this contract only when the reusable Ali–AI learning/execution rules change.

Do not add current milestone, current method, exact-next-action, session-specific instructions, or temporary implementation state.
