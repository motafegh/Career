# 90-Day Career Execution Contract

**Status:** Controlling  
**Owner:** Ali Rajabi  
**Preparation:** 2026-07-18 to 2026-07-19  
**Execution:** 2026-07-20 to 2026-10-17  
**Responsibility:** Stable workload, capacity, formal review, career-route change, and anti-diversion commitments

## 1. Boundary

This contract governs the 90-day career commitment. It does not govern ordinary UpgradePilot implementation, session procedure, technical decisions, current plans, or next actions.

UpgradePilot owns its project operation. Career is updated only when Ali explicitly requests a Career review or a durable career/program decision changes.

## 2. Working identity and allocation

> **AI-augmented Python/data/ML engineer developing secure engineering capability.**

- Python and data engineering are the implementation core.
- Machine learning is the analytical specialization.
- Security is an application and engineering-quality layer.
- AI is a working multiplier, not evidence of unassisted capability.
- UpgradePilot is the selected primary flagship.
- AegisLab, Sentinel, and other repositories remain historical evidence unless a formal Career decision assigns a bounded role.

## 3. Workload commitment

### Green mode

- Monday–Saturday: 4 focused hours per day.
- Standard week: 24 focused hours.
- Normal daily ceiling: 5 focused hours.

### Yellow mode

- 2.5 focused hours of safe, lower-risk work.
- The 1.5-hour difference may be recovered within the same week when safe.
- Do not make consequential architecture or strategic decisions when functioning is materially reduced.

### Red mode

- 0 required technical hours.
- Preserve project continuation and resume later.
- No unsafe compensatory overwork.

A Red day reduces the adjusted weekly target by four hours. Repeated Red days trigger a review when Ali requests or when health/safety requires reconsideration.

## 4. Time and quality

- Time estimates are ceilings and planning containers, not targets to fill.
- Gates and concrete evidence matter more than elapsed hours.
- Do not begin a new consequential responsibility merely to fill remaining time when concentration, comprehension, or diagnostic quality has materially declined.
- Safe remaining capacity may be used for review, reproduction, a small test, evidence organization, or stopping.
- Health, safety, legality, privacy, credentials, and cost override workload targets.

## 5. Career anti-diversion commitments

Until the formal Day-90 review, do not:

- reopen broad project comparison without explicit Career-review evidence;
- start an unapproved competing flagship;
- adopt a new career identity casually;
- turn a project blocker into a new general curriculum or roadmap without demonstrated need;
- treat historical projects as active because they already exist;
- claim professional mastery or production readiness without evidence;
- change approved advanced-system commitments through ordinary documentation cleanup.

Project-local technical exploration, ADRs, plans, and experiments are not prohibited when UpgradePilot authorizes and bounds them.

## 6. Formal review points

- Day 14 workload calibration: 2026-08-02;
- Day 30: 2026-08-18;
- Day 60: 2026-09-17;
- Day 90: 2026-10-17;
- any additional Career review explicitly requested by Ali.

A review may inspect UpgradePilot as deeply as necessary, but Career records only the resulting coarse state, capability assessment, capacity decision, role/strategy decision, and claim boundaries.

## 7. Career-route change criteria

A change to primary project allocation, working identity, major capacity, or approved strategic requirement requires evidence such as:

- demonstrated technical infeasibility;
- material external, legal, safety, privacy, credential, or cost constraint;
- repeated workload evidence showing the commitment is unrealistic;
- repeated evidence that the project cannot produce the intended career capability or portfolio value;
- an explicit formal review requested by Ali.

Any change must state the evidence, smallest necessary correction, preserved work, affected career outcome, and how the revision will be reviewed.

## 8. Career update rule

Do not update Career for:

- one UpgradePilot test or commit;
- one project session or working-memory entry;
- a project next-action change;
- an ordinary technical decision or ADR;
- a project sub-gate;
- routine project implementation progress.

Ali explicitly initiates Career review. During that review, Career inspects current UpgradePilot evidence and records only what belongs at career level.

## 9. Maintenance

Change this contract only when stable workload, capacity, review, career-route change, or anti-diversion commitments change.
