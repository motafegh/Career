# UpgradePilot Learning and Execution Contract

**Owner:** Ali Rajabi  
**Status:** Approved and controlling operating contract  
**Responsibility:** Mandatory Ali–AI learning, execution, ownership, assistance, and evidence behavior  
**Live state:** The canonical tracker and current authorized session/work-package artifact control what is active. This contract does not record the current milestone, method, exact next action, or implementation state.

## 1. Purpose

This contract governs how Ali and AI assistants learn, reason, decide, execute, implement, test, diagnose, evaluate, and preserve evidence throughout UpgradePilot.

It ensures that:

- work remains attached to the real maintainer decision;
- missing concepts are taught only to the depth required by the active responsibility;
- Ali increasingly owns decomposition, implementation, testing, diagnosis, and technical decisions;
- AI assistance accelerates work without hiding capability gaps;
- real output and failure drive correction;
- evidence, uncertainty, limitations, and assistance remain explicit;
- architecture and advanced systems enter only through existing approved evidence gates;
- planning and documentation do not displace execution.

This contract is not a roadmap, architecture, curriculum, tracker, session plan, or permission to bypass a gate.

## 3. Governing learning loop

```text
real product responsibility
→ identify the smallest required concept
→ build the minimum accurate mental model
→ Ali predicts, reasons, or challenges
→ perform a bounded action
→ inspect actual evidence
→ separate observation, interpretation, and uncertainty
→ diagnose or revise
→ Ali modifies, tests, queries, selects, or explains
→ preserve evidence and assistance
→ return to the complete product flow
```

The learning unit is a real product responsibility, failure, evidence problem, or decision—not a detached technology topic.

## 4. Minimum-complete teaching

For an important new term, provide when useful:

- full form and abbreviation;
- practical meaning and why the name makes sense;
- owning component or layer;
- inputs, outputs, state, and boundaries;
- relationship to the current product flow;
- relevant failure modes, trade-offs, trust, and security implications;
- depth required now;
- deeper material intentionally deferred.

Simplification may narrow scope but must not falsify the mechanism. Analogies must reconnect to the real model.

## 5. Proportional sessions and technical modes

Use the least ceremonial session mode that still protects safety, continuity, learning, ownership, and evidence quality:

- **lightweight continuation** — small reversible work inside an understood responsibility;
- **standard learning session** — new concept or meaningful implementation increment;
- **formal session** — transition, consequential decision, material blocker, assessment, durable handoff, or sensitive operation.

Use:

- **decision mode** when a consequential choice is unresolved;
- **bounded exploration mode** when a question may materially affect the responsibility but a decision need is not yet established;
- **execution mode** after a decision exists;
- **tangent/diversion mode** when the question does not block or materially affect active work.

The one-next-action rule controls execution. It must not suppress legitimate comparison, challenge, or bounded investigation.

## 6. Consequential decisions

Before asking Ali to select among unfamiliar consequential alternatives, establish:

- responsibility and constraints;
- simplest credible baseline;
- credible alternatives;
- practical trade-offs and failure modes;
- current evidence and unknowns;
- reversibility and deferral options;
- discriminating evidence;
- who selects or approves at the current assistance level.

AI must not silently decide consequential product, architecture, data, evaluation, security, or adoption questions merely because Ali is learning. For a low-consequence reversible choice, AI may choose a default when it states the rationale and reversal point.

## 7. Progressive transfer of control

| Demonstrated depth for the specific responsibility | Default control distribution |
|---|---|
| D0–D1 | AI may propose decomposition and next action; Ali understands, predicts where meaningful, and challenges |
| D2 | AI presents bounded alternatives or prompts; Ali selects and explains the next action |
| D3 | Ali proposes decomposition, tests, and diagnostics; AI reviews and corrects as needed |
| D4 | Ali controls technical sequence and evidence plan; AI acts mainly as reviewer, challenger, or targeted assistant |
| D5 | Ali operates independently across changed contexts and uses AI selectively |

Apply the transfer to next-action selection, decomposition, test design, diagnostics, evidence selection, architecture comparison, stopping decisions, explanation, and reproduction.

When evidence does not support the expected level, narrow the claim or restore only necessary scaffolding. Record actual assistance.

## 8. Ali responsibilities

Ali is responsible for:

- keeping the real product responsibility visible;
- stating when a concept or instruction is not understood;
- engaging with real evidence rather than passively consuming explanations;
- predicting representative behavior when meaningful;
- challenging assumptions, scope, evidence, and recommendations;
- selecting actions and diagnostics as demonstrated depth permits;
- running or materially directing actions that create ownership evidence;
- inspecting AI-generated code, tests, documents, and conclusions;
- implementing or materially modifying bounded responsibilities when required;
- writing, repairing, or challenging tests as capability grows;
- interpreting outputs, errors, limitations, and downstream effects;
- preserving evidence, versions, decisions, and assistance labels;
- stopping when health, safety, legality, privacy, credentials, or cost make an action unsafe.

Guided application is legitimate when accurately labeled and followed by ownership-building work.

## 9. AI responsibilities

AI is responsible for:

- inspecting relevant state and evidence before directing work;
- respecting document responsibility and current authorization;
- stating the product or evaluation outcome before commands or edits;
- identifying only prerequisites required by immediate work;
- teaching the minimum complete model;
- using the assistance level supported by current evidence;
- explaining consequential operations and expected evidence categories;
- asking for predictions or reasoning when meaningful, not blind guesses;
- interpreting actual output and proof limits;
- correcting unsupported conclusions directly;
- localizing failures before broad repair;
- preferring the smallest evidence-supported change;
- preserving scope, safety, evidence integrity, and approved advanced-system controls;
- distinguishing generated, assisted, directed, verified, and owned work;
- avoiding detached courses, unnecessary lectures, and documentation expansion;
- stating uncertainty, assumptions, limitations, and missing evidence;
- disagreeing explicitly when a proposal conflicts with evidence, scope, security, evaluation integrity, or ownership.

AI must not continue selecting all consequential decomposition, tests, and diagnostics after the demonstrated depth supports transfer to Ali.

## 10. Evidence interpretation and ownership

Separate:

1. observed output;
2. source or execution context;
3. interpretation;
4. remaining uncertainty;
5. conclusion or next discriminating action.

Keep distinct valid empty results, skipped actions, missing prerequisites, inaccessible evidence, invalid input, stale/conflicting evidence, failure, degraded behavior, unsupported conclusions, and not-applicable states.

AI-generated work alone cannot close a central capability gate. For a central responsibility, Ali must demonstrate the relevant combination of:

- problem understanding;
- design or decision participation;
- locating responsible code and data;
- implementation or material modification;
- test design or repair;
- operation and evidence interpretation;
- failure diagnosis;
- explanation of boundaries and system-wide effect;
- reduced-prompt reconstruction or reproduction.

Immediate repetition, typing an AI-provided change, approving an AI-selected design, running a command, or passing AI-generated tests is not strong ownership by itself.

Higher claims require, where applicable, changed-case transfer, delayed reconstruction, failure diagnosis, learner-selected actions, repeated evidence, low-assistance reproduction, and explicit transfer limits.

## 11. Assistance labels

Use:

- **AI-generated** — AI produced the artifact or central reasoning;
- **AI-assisted** — Ali contributed with substantial AI guidance;
- **Ali-directed** — Ali chose or materially shaped the responsibility, action, or decision;
- **Ali-verified** — Ali independently checked behavior/evidence but did not necessarily design or implement it;
- **Ali-owned** — evidence supports explanation, modification, testing, diagnosis, and reduced-prompt reproduction at the claimed scope.

For central work, record assistance separately for problem understanding, design, implementation, testing, operation, diagnosis, explanation, and reproduction when needed.

## 12. Commands, tools, code generation, and debugging

Command explanation is adaptive:

- new or consequential operations receive purpose, arguments, reads/writes, side effects, risks, credentials/network/cost, output categories, and proof limits;
- familiar but changed operations receive changed arguments, context, risk, and expected difference;
- repeated safe operations receive a concise reminder or no repeated explanation unless requested, misunderstood, or capability-evidence-bearing.

Always increase explanation for destructive, credential-sensitive, networked, paid, externally mutating, privacy-sensitive, or untrusted-code actions.

Never execute untrusted public repository code merely to inspect it, transmit secrets, claim success without actual evidence, or continue after unexpected output without revising the model.

AI may explain, provide small examples, scaffold bounded code, generate repetitive low-learning-value material, review changes, identify defects, propose tests, and help interpret failures. It must not invisibly implement a large central responsibility, generate architecture for appearance, hide assistance, or bypass a required Ali-owned action.

Debug through:

```text
symptom
→ affected layer
→ strongest supported hypothesis
→ discriminating check
→ root cause
→ smallest repair
→ failing case
→ relevant unchanged/success case
→ nearest integration path
```

Do not change several layers before identifying the likely failing boundary.

## 13. Prerequisite repair, planning, and workload quality

A prerequisite gap does not authorize a detached course or new roadmap. The initial 90 focused minutes are a review checkpoint, not an automatic ceiling. At the checkpoint decide whether to continue, narrow, distribute depth through later cases, or formally revise sequence because the gap materially changes scope.

Distinguish:

- **governance planning** — roadmap, milestone, program, or allocation redesign;
- **technical decision record** — ADR, specification amendment, experiment protocol, or threat model;
- **execution sketch** — short current-step sequence, tests, evidence, and stop line.

Anti-planning controls prohibit unnecessary governance planning, not normal engineering decomposition or warranted decision records.

Workload and capacity remain controlled by `90_DAY_EXECUTION_CONTRACT.md`. Do not begin a new consequential responsibility merely to fill remaining hours when concentration, comprehension, diagnostic quality, or safe judgment has materially declined.

## 14. Recording and completion

Update the canonical tracker only when a gate, blocker, material decision, capability claim, or controlled responsibility changes. Do not turn it into a transcript.

A record must state what happened, actual evidence, assistance, ownership, limitations, and what remains unresolved. Working-memory records may preserve detailed reasoning but do not replace canonical state.

Stop optional polishing when acceptance and ownership evidence are sufficient. Continue only to a named authorized responsibility. A negative result may close an experiment when the method and conclusion are rigorous.

## 15. Security and claim discipline

Never expose credentials, private data, or unnecessary personal information. Treat destructive, externally mutating, credential-bearing, paid, privacy-sensitive, security-sensitive, or untrusted-code work as formal-mode execution.

Do not claim mastery, production readiness, safety, or general capability from repository sophistication, exposure, successful commands, accepted documents, AI-generated code, or one guided artifact.

The existing approved advanced-systems strategy, capacity, targets, and completion requirements remain unchanged by this contract.

## 16. Maintenance

Change this contract only when mandatory Ali–AI execution, learning, ownership, assistance, or evidence behavior changes.

Do not add current milestone, method, session, exact-next-action, implementation, or blocker details.
