# Career Strategy and Scope

**Status:** Approved stable career strategy  
**Owner:** Ali Rajabi  
**Responsibility:** Working identity, role direction, capability priorities, flagship allocation, advanced-system career strategy, and claim boundaries

## 1. Working identity

> **AI-augmented Python/data/ML engineer developing secure engineering capability.**

This identity is intentionally narrower than Ali's long-term hybrid-engineering ambition and remains fixed until a formal Career review changes it.

## 2. Flagship allocation

UpgradePilot is the selected primary flagship for the 90-day period.

Career records the allocation and career expectations only. UpgradePilot owns project mission details, operation, route, milestones, plans, specifications, ADRs, source, tests, and current continuation.

AegisLab and Sentinel remain historical evidence sources. Their architecture, sequencing, modules, and tasks do not transfer automatically.

## 3. Primary capability direction

Build reliable Python systems that transform real raw data into validated, queryable, tested, explainable, and reproducible outputs.

Specialization:

- sound data and machine-learning methods when they add measured value over explicit baselines;
- secure engineering, evidence quality, and operational reliability as product responsibilities;
- AI-assisted implementation with honest human understanding, verification, direction, and ownership.

## 4. Capability priorities

1. Python application structure and implementation ownership.
2. Testing and evidence-based debugging.
3. Real-input acquisition, parsing, validation, and provenance.
4. Data modeling, persistence, and SQL.
5. Reproducible end-to-end pipelines and user-facing operation.
6. Secure engineering and trustworthy evidence handling.
7. Docker/CI and operational reproducibility when justified.
8. Baselines, evaluation, and honest ML/AI experimentation.
9. Project-connected advanced-system exposure.
10. Technical explanation and portfolio presentation.

## 6. Advanced-system strategy

The approved minimum remains:

- A1 hands-on exposure for distributed queues, advanced MLOps, microservices, Kubernetes, multi-cloud, and bounded autonomous multi-agent systems;
- at least two A2 project-integrated pilots unless an explicit strategic review changes the requirement;
- A3 permanent adoption only when project evidence justifies it.

The detailed exposure/adoption definitions remain controlled by `ADVANCED_SYSTEMS_EXPOSURE_AND_ADOPTION_POLICY.md`.

UpgradePilot owns the actual workloads, implementation, failures, cleanup, comparisons, and decisions. Career evaluates the evidence only during an explicit review.

## 7. Geographic and work-mode direction

Priority order:

1. English-language roles in the UAE and broader Asia region;
2. hybrid or on-site roles with real engineering mentorship;
3. structured remote roles with code review and supervision;
4. fully remote unsupervised roles only when requirements closely match demonstrated capability.

Review against real job descriptions during the Day-60 and Day-90 Career reviews.

## 8. Evidence and claim standard

Career claims require evidence that Ali can perform a specific responsibility at the stated assistance level and transfer scope.

Do not infer capability from:

- documentation volume;
- repository sophistication;
- technology exposure;
- immediate repetition;
- successful commands;
- AI-generated implementation;
- passing AI-generated tests.

Use the formal Capability and Evidence Model during explicit Career reviews.

## 9. Review boundary

Career is updated only when Ali explicitly requests a review or a durable career/program decision changes.

A review may inspect UpgradePilot source, tests, plans, memory, evidence, decisions, and portfolio artifacts, but Career records only:

- coarse project state;
- capability conclusions and transfer limits;
- workload/capacity decision;
- role/strategy decision;
- portfolio claim boundaries;
- next Career review trigger.

Do not copy project next actions or implementation details into Career.

## 10. Historical inputs

`LEARNING_AND_PROJECT_DESIGN_PROFILE.md` and `PROJECT_SELECTION_AND_CAPABILITY_SPECIFICATION.md` preserve pre-selection reasoning. They are historical inputs and do not reopen project selection or control UpgradePilot operation.

## 11. Change control

Change this strategy only when working identity, role direction, capability priorities, flagship allocation, geographic direction, advanced-system strategy, or Career claim standards change.
