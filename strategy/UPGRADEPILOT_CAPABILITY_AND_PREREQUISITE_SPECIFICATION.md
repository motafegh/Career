# Career Capability and Evidence Model

**Status:** Approved controlling capability-assessment model  
**Owner:** Ali Rajabi  
**Responsibility:** D0–D5 depth, evidence standards, assistance, transfer limits, prerequisite interpretation, and Career-review records

## 1. Boundary

Use this model during an explicit Career review or when Ali asks for a formal capability assessment.

It does not:

- control UpgradePilot sessions or next actions;
- require Career updates after ordinary project work;
- define project requirements or architecture;
- turn every learning event into an administrative record.

UpgradePilot preserves project evidence locally. Career inspects that evidence when a review is explicitly initiated.

## 2. Assessment principles

1. Assess a specific responsibility, not a broad topic.
2. Record what Ali can demonstrate, not what was mentioned, generated, or successfully run.
3. Keep exposure, guided application, independent bounded application, technical ownership, and advanced independent capability distinct.
4. Record actual assistance and transfer limits.
5. Require changed-case, failure, delayed, and reduced-prompt evidence only when the claimed depth requires them.
6. Do not average mixed sub-capabilities into an optimistic broad label.
7. Documentation volume, repository sophistication, successful commands, and passing AI-generated tests do not establish ownership.
8. Advanced-system A-level exposure labels do not substitute for D-level capability evidence.

## 3. Depth model

| Depth | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **D0 — Unassessed** | Reliable ability has not been established. |
| **D1 — Introduced** | Ali recognizes the responsibility, practical purpose, main boundary, and relationship to the project after teaching. |
| **D2 — Guided application** | Ali performs a bounded real or representative task with substantial explanation, prompts, examples, or correction and interprets the evidence correctly. |
| **D3 — Independent bounded application** | Ali performs a representative bounded responsibility with limited assistance, selects meaningful actions, handles a changed case, and diagnoses a common relevant failure. |
| **D4 — Technical ownership** | Ali can design or challenge the responsibility, implement or materially modify it, test it, diagnose unfamiliar failures, explain trade-offs/system effects, and reproduce it with low assistance across relevant contexts. |
| **D5 — Advanced independent capability** | Ali repeatedly designs, operates, diagnoses, and evolves the responsibility across materially different contexts and substantial trade-offs with low assistance. |

D5 is not required for the current 90-day core. D2 or D3 may be an honest result for bounded experiments or advanced exposure.

## 4. Evidence by depth

### D1

Require:

- own-words practical meaning;
- relationship to the project flow;
- one important boundary or failure distinction.

### D2

Require:

- guided real or representative application;
- correct interpretation of output/evidence;
- explicit assistance;
- one bounded check, modification, query, or test where applicable;
- narrow transfer limit.

### D3

Require, where applicable:

- representative project case;
- changed case or variant;
- meaningful action/test/query selected with limited prompting;
- relevant failure diagnosis or repair;
- ownership-bearing change;
- delayed recall or reconstruction;
- explicit context and transfer limit.

One immediate guided success cannot establish D3.

### D4

Require:

- repeated evidence across sessions or materially different contexts;
- design/challenge participation;
- implementation and test ownership;
- unfamiliar-failure diagnosis;
- system-wide trade-off explanation;
- low-assistance reproduction;
- explicit limitations.

### D5

Require sustained independent performance across materially different contexts, unfamiliar failures, consequential trade-offs, and operational effects.

## 5. Record tiers

### 5.1 Lean record — default

Use for most formal Career assessments:

```text
Responsibility:
Depth:
Best evidence:
Assistance:
Last demonstrated:
Transfer limit:
Next reassessment:
```

Do not create a separate Career record for every small project step. Combine evidence only when it supports the same bounded responsibility.

### 5.2 Extended record — exceptional

Use only for:

- central milestone capabilities;
- D3 or higher claims;
- disputed assessments;
- mixed ownership;
- portfolio/job claims;
- formal review where breadth, confidence, or freshness is material.

Additional fields may include:

```text
Context:
Ownership dimensions:
Changed-case evidence:
Failure evidence:
Delayed evidence:
Breadth:
Confidence:
Freshness:
Reassessment trigger:
```

## 6. Ownership dimensions

When an extended record is justified, assess only the dimensions material to the claimed responsibility:

- problem understanding;
- design;
- implementation;
- testing;
- operation;
- diagnosis;
- explanation;
- reproduction.

Do not collapse mixed ownership into one optimistic label.

## 7. Assistance labels

- **AI-generated** — AI produced substantive artifact, implementation, or central reasoning.
- **AI-assisted** — Ali performed meaningful work with substantial AI explanation, generation, or repair.
- **Ali-directed** — Ali materially defined the responsibility, constraints, approach, or decision.
- **Ali-verified** — Ali independently checked behavior/evidence and stated proof and limits correctly.
- **Ali-owned** — evidence supports explanation, modification, testing, diagnosis, and reduced-prompt reproduction at the stated scope.

Participation labels do not replace D-level evidence.

## 8. Performative-check prohibition

The following alone cannot establish D3+ or ownership:

- repeating an explanation immediately after AI;
- typing an AI-provided change;
- approving an AI-selected design;
- running a command successfully;
- passing AI-generated tests;
- producing one guided artifact;
- recognizing terminology;
- reading source and agreeing with its behavior.

## 9. Prerequisite interpretation

UpgradePilot's Operating Guide controls project-local prerequisite repair.

During Career review, assess whether a prerequisite gap is:

- required for the current career claim;
- supporting operational knowledge;
- important but deferred;
- optional exploration.

A missing prerequisite lowers or narrows the claim. It does not automatically create a new curriculum or project route.

## 10. Career review workflow

When Ali requests a capability review:

1. define the career-level question and specific responsibilities;
2. inspect relevant UpgradePilot evidence rather than copying all project state;
3. distinguish product completion from capability evidence;
4. choose lean or extended records proportionally;
5. record assistance, transfer limit, and next reassessment;
6. update Career only with the resulting formal assessment.

## 11. Claim rules

- A broad family such as Python, testing, security, or data engineering cannot receive one undifferentiated depth.
- A high-confidence D1 remains D1.
- A narrow D3 does not imply broad professional independence.
- Capability may remain valid within its demonstrated scope even when a new context requires assistance.
- Stale evidence requires reassessment before a strong current claim.

## 12. Change control

Change this model only when depth definitions, formal evidence standards, record tiers, assistance labels, ownership interpretation, or Career-review method changes.

Do not update it for ordinary UpgradePilot progress.
