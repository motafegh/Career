UpgradePilot must provide credible, mission-connected pathways to:

- Kubernetes;
- a microservice boundary;
- a distributed queue;

- a bounded autonomous multi-agent workflow;
- an advanced MLOps lifecycle.

The minimum target is A1 hands-on exposure for all six and A2 project-integrated pilots for at least two, subject to evidence and feasibility rules.

- broad DFIR expansion;
- broad threat-hunting expansion;
- dynamic malware work;
- additional unrelated ecosystems or user workflows.

### Sentinel — research archive

Sentinel is preserved as AI-assisted experimental research and prior evidence.

It is not:

- the primary project;
- a source of unreviewed requirements;
- proof of full personal ownership;
- permitted to create active tasks or dictate UpgradePilot architecture, sequence, modules, or roadmap.

Selectively reusable acquisition, provenance, representation, evaluation, and scope-control lessons may enter only through explicit UpgradePilot admission.

### Older projects — inactive

Older repositories remain historical evidence unless an approved UpgradePilot plan or formal review explicitly reuses a bounded component or lesson.

By Day 90, the portfolio must demonstrate a bounded core product plus accurately labeled advanced-systems exposure.

Required core evidence:

- a real user problem and supported decision;
- runnable Python package or clearly structured application;
- real-input acquisition path;
- raw-to-normalized data path;
- validation and explicit degraded or rejected behavior;
- persistence and useful SQL queries where justified;
- transparent analytical baseline;
- unit and integration tests;
- Docker or another justified reproducible runtime path;
- CI;
- reproducible end-to-end demonstration;
- deliberate failure diagnosis;
- honest ML comparison when supported;
- honest README and limitations;
- clear disclosure of AI assistance;
- Ali explanation and modification of important components.

Required advanced-systems evidence:

- A1 hands-on exposure to Kubernetes;
- A1 hands-on exposure to a microservice boundary;
- A1 hands-on exposure to a distributed queue;
- A1 hands-on exposure to multi-cloud deployment or portability;
- A1 hands-on exposure to an autonomous multi-agent workflow;
- A1 hands-on exposure to an advanced MLOps lifecycle;
- A2 project-integrated pilot evidence for at least two areas;
- explicit adopt, retain-as-pilot, reject, or defer decisions;
- clear distinction between exposure, adopted architecture, and mastery.

3. preserve evidence and update the active tracker;

5. do not reopen candidate comparison without formal change evidence;
6. do not create another planning artifact before M2-S01 execution;
7. do not begin code, architecture, corpus, model, or advanced-system work outside the active gate.
